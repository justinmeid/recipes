## Instructions

1. Heat the oven to 400°F. Pat the chicken thighs dry and season with 1 tsp each salt,
   pepper, and oregano plus a pinch of crushed red pepper.
2. Heat the oil in a large Dutch oven over medium-high. Add the thighs skin-side down and
   cook undisturbed until they self-release, about 5 minutes. Remove and set aside.
3. Cut 1 lemon into ¼-inch slices. Add to the pot and cook until caramelized, about 2
   minutes; remove and set aside.
4. Add the olives, garlic, shallot, and another 1 tsp each salt, pepper, and oregano. Cook
   over medium-low, scraping up browned bits, until fragrant, 2–3 minutes. Turn heat to
   high, add the rice and broth, stir, cover, and bring to a boil, about 5 minutes.
5. Off the heat, nestle the thighs skin-side up on the rice and top with the caramelized
   lemon slices. Cover and bake 25–30 minutes until the rice and chicken are cooked through.
6. Serve topped with parsley and a squeeze of juice from the second lemon.
