## Instructions

1. Turn the heat to high under a large, deep skillet and add the beef a little at a time,
   breaking it into small pieces. Stir and break it up more, then add the chickpeas (reserve
   1 cup of their liquid). Keep the heat high and cook, stirring occasionally, until the
   chickpeas begin to brown and pop, 5–10 minutes. Lower the heat slightly if it scorches.
2. Add the cumin, chile, and garlic and cook, stirring, about 1 minute. Add the reserved
   chickpea liquid and stir, scraping up any browned bits. Season with salt and pepper, then
   lower the heat to medium-low and cook until the mixture is no longer soupy but not dry.
3. Stir in the olive oil, taste, and adjust the seasoning. Garnish if you like and serve
   with rice or pita.

## Notes

Ground turkey, chicken, or pork all sub in well (add a little oil to the pan first). For
more kick, double or triple the ancho chile or chile powder.
